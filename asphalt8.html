    Asphalt 8: Airborne is a racing game developed and published by Gameloft, the tenth game in the Asphalt series. It was released on August 22, 2013 for iOS and Android, and on November 13 for Windows Phone, Windows 8 and 10. The game currently has more than 300 cars and motorcycles, as well as more than 75 circuits in 21 locations, which have been added through updates. The cover car was initially the Lamborghini Veneno, but is currently the Lamborghini Centenario. Compared to its predecessors, Asphalt 8 had more success and marketing, many media believe this was due to a growing increase in the mobile market and the use of social networks. Like Asphalt 7, it is possible to configure by gyroscope or touch screen in 4 schemes for mobile devices and tablets, by keyboard keys in the case of PCs with Windows 8 or higher, or controls such as gamepads or steering wheels. As the name implies, leave chases behind to enter the jumps, normally (if a straight ramp is used), helicopter (if you drift before using a ramp), or barrel roll (if a curved ramp is used). These last 2 types of jumping in the air increase nitro, the same goes for a perfect section (no accidents), knocking down enemies or obstacles and skidding, as well as recharging nitro on the track. Also, if the nitro is activated and reaches the red zone, it is triggered again to activate the "perfect nitro", replacing the "adrenaline" mode used in Asphalt 6 and 7. The nitro stops working if you run out of nitro , brake, crash, go off the track or use the ramp. With the exception of Quick Race mode, the chance of earning random credits, whether from maneuvers, takedowns, or destruction, is increased. Credits, tokens, fusion coins and car packages can also be purchased using credit and debit cards (Internet connection required).
